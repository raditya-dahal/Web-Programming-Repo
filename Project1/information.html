Child Development Information

Comprehensive details about children's growth stages, milestones, interests, health, and development.

Caregiver & Staff Information Form

Age Stages and Milestones

+

Infancy (0-12 months)

  • Lifts head briefly during tummy time
  • Responds to sounds and familiar voices
  • Begins to babble and make vowel sounds
  • Sits without support
  • Develops pincer grasp

Toddler (1-3 years)

  • Begins walking independently
  • Uses simple words and phrases
  • Follows simple instructions
  • Engages in pretend play
  • Shows independence

Preschool (3-5 years)

  • Uses complete sentences
  • Plays cooperatively with others
  • Draws recognizable shapes and figures
  • Understands basic concepts (colors, numbers)
  • Follows multi-step directions

Interests and Hobbies

+

Children's interests evolve as they grow. Understanding these can help in providing appropriate stimulation:

Early Childhood (2-4 years)

  • Sensory play (water, sand, playdough)
  • Simple puzzles and matching games
  • Repetitive songs and rhymes
  • Building with large blocks

Middle Childhood (5-8 years)

  • Imaginative and pretend play
  • Simple board games
  • Arts and crafts
  • Beginning sports activities

Later Childhood (9-12 years)

  • More complex games and puzzles
  • Team sports
  • Reading for pleasure
  • Developing specific skill-based hobbies

Medications and Allergies

+

Common Childhood Allergies

  • Food allergies: Milk, eggs, peanuts, tree nuts, soy, wheat, fish, shellfish
  • Environmental allergies: Pollen, dust mites, mold, pet dander
  • Insect sting allergies: Bees, wasps, hornets

Common Childhood Medications

  • Fever reducers: Acetaminophen, ibuprofen (age-appropriate)
  • Allergy medications: Antihistamines, nasal sprays
  • Antibiotics: For bacterial infections (always complete full course)

Important: Always consult with a pediatrician before administering any medication to children.

Growth and Health Insights

+

Physical Growth Patterns

Children grow at different rates, but generally follow these patterns:

  • Rapid growth in the first year (triple birth weight)
  • Steady growth during toddler and preschool years
  • Growth spurts during middle childhood
  • Adolescent growth spurt (puberty)

Nutritional Needs

  • Balanced diet with variety of fruits, vegetables, whole grains, proteins
  • Adequate calcium for bone development
  • Iron-rich foods to prevent anemia
  • Appropriate portion sizes for age

Sleep Requirements

  • Newborns (0-3 months): 14-17 hours
  • Infants (4-11 months): 12-15 hours
  • Toddlers (1-2 years): 11-14 hours
  • Preschoolers (3-5 years): 10-13 hours
  • School-age (6-13 years): 9-11 hours
